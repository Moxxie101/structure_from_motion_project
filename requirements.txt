```
### The One Thing Most Beginners Miss

**Camera intrinsics matter enormously.** Every reconstruction assumes you know the focal length and principal point of your camera. Without them, the math is underconstrained.

Your three options, in order of preference:

1. **Read EXIF data** from photos — most smartphones embed focal length. Use `Pillow` or `exifread` to extract it.
2. **Calibrate your camera** with a checkerboard pattern using `cv2.calibrateCamera()` — do this once per lens and save the matrix.
3. **Let COLMAP estimate it** — if you use `pycolmap`, it can estimate intrinsics from scratch as part of reconstruction, but results are less stable.

The camera intrinsic matrix `K` looks like this and feeds into almost every calculation in your pipeline:
```
K = [[fx,  0, cx],
     [ 0, fy, cy],
     [ 0,  0,  1]]

# fx, fy = focal length in pixels
# cx, cy = principal point (usually image center)
